Our Mission

Find Deal Vault was born from a simple question: "How much does this actually cost to make?" That question led us down a rabbit hole of industry reports, teardown analyses, wholesale pricing data, and investigative journalism — and what we found shocked us.

A ₹25,000 designer handbag that costs ₹8,000 to manufacture. A ₹350 coffee that uses ₹18 worth of ingredients. A ₹1,900 phone cable that costs ₹150 to produce. The pattern was consistent: brands are making enormous profits while consumers remain in the dark about true production costs.

We're not anti-business or anti-profit. Companies need to cover R&D, marketing, distribution, and make a reasonable return. But when markups reach 1,000%, 5,000%, or even 8,000% — as you'll see in our vault — something has gone wrong in the transparency of our markets.

Our goal isn't to tell you what to buy or not buy. It's simply to provide the information that should be publicly available but isn't. Armed with this knowledge, you can make more informed decisions, negotiate better, or simply appreciate the psychology behind why we pay what we pay.

Every product in our vault is backed by research from credible sources. We show our methodology, cite our sources, and clearly state when figures are estimates. We believe in radical transparency — because that's exactly what's missing from the pricing of most consumer goods.

How We Calculate Costs

Source Gathering

We collect data from industry reports, product teardowns, wholesale pricing, and investigative journalism across multiple countries.

Cost Estimation

We calculate material, labor, and basic overhead costs. These are estimates representing typical production, not exact figures.

Markup Calculation

Markup = ((Retail Price − Cost Price) / Cost Price) × 100. Simple math, shocking results every time.

Verification

We cross-reference multiple sources and update figures as new data becomes available. Sources are cited per product.
